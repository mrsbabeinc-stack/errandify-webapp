## 🎁 What Are Ad Credits?

### How They Work

Companies get a **monthly ad budget** to spend on Errandify advertising:

**They can use credits for:**
- ✅ Banner ads (SGD $50-200 per placement)
- ✅ In-feed ads (SGD $30-100 per week)
- ✅ Featured listings
- ✅ Campaign promotions
- ✅ Weekend specials

**Example (Silver company with SGD $50/month):**
```
Month 1: Spend $50 on banner ad → Gets 5,000 impressions
Month 2: Save $50 + $50 new = $100 → Run in-feed ads
Month 3: Use credits to promote weekend special
```

### Why Ad Credits Are Better Than EP

| Aspect | EP Allowance | Ad Credits |
|--------|---|---|
| **Cost to you** | SGD $0.10 per point (external redemption) | Ad server cost (already built) |
| **Where spent** | External discounts (loses them) | On YOUR platform (keeps engagement) |
| **Profit margin** | Negative (you lose money) | Profitable (drives revenue) |
| **Engagement** | One-time use | Recurring monthly (stickiness) |
| **Platform benefit** | None (they leave) | High (more ads = visibility) |
| **How it scales** | Expensive as you grow | Scales with infrastructure |

## 🚀 How Ad Credits Work Technically

### For Users
```
1. User subscribes to Silver ($29/month)
2. System allocates SGD $50 ad credit
3. User logs into advertising dashboard
4. Selects: Banner ad for 2 weeks
5. Spends $50 credit
6. Banner runs automatically
7. Next month: $50 new credit resets (unused doesn't roll over)
```

### Backend Logic Needed
1. **Subscription status tracking** (active tier)
2. **Monthly credit allocation** (SGD $50/200/500 on billing date)
3. **Credit balance tracking** (per company)
4. **Ad campaign cost deduction** (subtract from balance)
5. **No rollover** (unused credits expire monthly)
6. **Dashboard showing:** Balance, usage history, campaigns

### Database Tables
```sql
-- Subscription info
company_subscriptions (id, company_id, tier, status, billing_date)

-- Ad credits
subscription_ad_credits (id, subscription_id, month, amount_allocated, amount_used)

-- Campaign cost tracking
ad_campaigns (id, company_id, cost, status, dates)
```
