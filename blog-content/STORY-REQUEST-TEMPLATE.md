## INTERVIEW GUIDE (For When They Say Yes)

### Pre-Interview Checklist

- [ ] Confirmed availability (date/time/platform: Zoom/WhatsApp call)
- [ ] Explained process ("We'll chat, then write your story for you to review")
- [ ] Explained anonymity option
- [ ] Sent article examples so they know what to expect
- [ ] Got permission: "Can I record this for accuracy?"

### Interview Structure (20-30 minutes)

**Opening (2 min)**
"Thanks so much for sharing. I'm going to ask about your situation and experience. Nothing is off-limits—if you don't want something shared, we won't share it. Ready?"

**The Story (15-20 min)**

1. **Background (3 min)**
   - "Tell me a bit about yourself—what do you do?"
   - "How many kids do you have?"
   - "What's your work situation now?"

2. **The Challenge (5 min)**
   - "What was the main challenge you faced?"
   - "When did you realize something had to change?"
   - "How did you feel about it?"
   - *Listen for: emotion, specifics, timeline*

3. **Finding Solutions (5 min)**
   - "How did you figure out what to do?"
   - "What resources helped you?"
   - "Were you surprised by anything?"
   - *Listen for: research process, government programs, community help*

4. **Taking Action (3 min)**
   - "Walk me through what you actually did"
   - "What was hardest?"
   - "What surprised you?"
   - *Listen for: specific steps, timeline, honest challenges*

5. **Outcome (2 min)**
   - "How are things now?"
   - "What would you tell someone in your old situation?"
   - "Anything you wish you'd known?"

**Closing (2 min)**
- "Anything else you want to add?"
- "Can we use your name/photo?" (confirm again)
- "When can we send you the draft to review?"

### Recording & Notes

- Record audio (with permission)
- Take notes on emotional moments/good quotes
- Capture exact numbers (if they share them)
- Get follow-up clarifications in writing via email

### Post-Interview

- Thank them (in moment + follow-up email)
- Send draft within 1 week for review
- Request approval before publishing
- Send them final article link to share
- Thank them publicly (on social media, if they want)

## MANAGING SENSITIVE STORIES

**If someone shares sensitive topic (abuse, financial crisis, health):**

✅ **DO:**
- Treat with respect and care
- Ask what's okay to share
- Offer full anonymity
- Let them review draft carefully
- Validate their experience

❌ **DON'T:**
- Sensationalize the story
- Add details they didn't share
- Publish without clear approval
- Identify them if they want anonymity

**Red flag stories** (might need extra care):
- Domestic violence/abuse
- Severe financial hardship
- Mental health crisis
- Discrimination/harassment

Always offer full anonymity + thorough review for these.
