# Subscription ROI - With Maintenance & Feature Costs

## The Real Picture

When you give someone a subscription, **ROI isn't just commission savings + EP**. You have to factor in **what it costs YOU to maintain all those features**.

---

## 💰 What Costs You Money

### 1. **Server & Infrastructure Costs** (Per User)
- Database storage
- API calls
- Processing power
- Backup systems

**Typical SaaS:** SGD $2-5/user/month

### 2. **Feature Maintenance** (Shared Cost)
- Bug fixes
- Updates
- Security patches
- Performance optimization

**Shared across all users, but subscription users use MORE features**

### 3. **Support Cost** (Per User)
- Email support (Silver/Gold)
- Priority support (Gold/Platinum)
- Strategy calls (Platinum)

| Tier | Support Type | Cost/Month |
|------|---|---|
| Silver | Best-effort email | SGD $2-3 |
| Gold | Priority email | SGD $5-8 |
| Platinum | Dedicated manager + calls | SGD $15-25 |

### 4. **Payment Processing** (Per Transaction)
- Stripe fees: 2.2% + SGD $0.30 per charge
- Subscription processing: SGD $0.30/transaction

---

## 📊 Real Subscription Unit Economics

### 🥈 SILVER ($24/month)

**Revenue per user/month:** SGD $24

**Costs per user/month:**
- Server/infrastructure: $3
- Support: $2
- Payment processing: $0.30
- **Subtotal: $5.30**

**Gross margin:** $24 - $5.30 = **$18.70/month ($224/year)**

**But you also give them:**
- 300 EP/month (SGD $30 cost to you)
- **Net margin:** $18.70 - $30 = **-$11.30/month (LOSS)**

**With commission savings (at SGD $5K revenue):**
- Commission savings to them: $1,200/year ✓
- Their subscription cost: $288/year
- Their net benefit: $912/year

**For YOU:**
- Subscription revenue: $288/year
- Your costs: ($5.30 × 12) + ($30 × 12) = $426/year
- Your net: $288 - $426 = **-$138/year (LOSS PER CUSTOMER)**

---

### 🥇 GOLD ($69/month)

**Revenue per user/month:** SGD $69

**Costs per user/month:**
- Server/infrastructure: $4
- Support (priority): $7
- Payment processing: $0.50
- Feature complexity (P&L reports, API, etc.): $5
- **Subtotal: $16.50**

**Gross margin:** $69 - $16.50 = **$52.50/month ($630/year)**

**But you also give them:**
- 1,000 EP/month (SGD $100 cost to you)
- **Net margin:** $52.50 - $100 = **-$47.50/month (LOSS)**

**Annual loss:** -$570/year per customer

---

### 💎 PLATINUM ($129/month)

**Revenue per user/month:** SGD $129

**Costs per user/month:**
- Server/infrastructure: $5
- Support (dedicated manager + 1 call/month): $25
- Payment processing: $0.70
- Feature complexity (payroll, recruitment, API, etc.): $15
- **Subtotal: $45.70**

**Gross margin:** $129 - $45.70 = **$83.30/month ($1,000/year)**

**But you also give them:**
- 2,500 EP/month (SGD $250 cost to you)
- **Net margin:** $83.30 - $250 = **-$166.70/month (LOSS)**

**Annual loss:** -$2,000/year per customer

---

## 🚨 THE PROBLEM

**You're losing money on every subscription tier!**

| Tier | Monthly Price | Monthly Cost | Monthly Loss | Annual Loss |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Silver | $24 | $33.30 | -$9.30 | -$111.60 |
| Gold | $69 | $116.50 | -$47.50 | -$570 |
| Platinum | $129 | $295.70 | -$166.70 | -$2,000 |

**The EP allowance is TOO HIGH compared to subscription revenue.**

---

## ✅ SOLUTION 1: Reduce EP Dramatically

If you want subscriptions to break even (not be a loss):

| Tier | Price | COGS | Safe EP/Month | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Silver** | $24 | $5.30 | **50 EP** (SGD $5) | Just a bonus, not the main perk |
| **Gold** | $69 | $16.50 | **400 EP** (SGD $40) | Still valuable |
| **Platinum** | $129 | $45.70 | **700 EP** (SGD $70) | Reasonable for enterprise |

**This way each subscription actually makes money.**

---

## ✅ SOLUTION 2: Stop Giving EP as "Free Money"

Instead of **"free EP allowance"**, change it to:

**"EP Credit toward ad campaigns"**
- Silver: SGD $50/month ad credit (not personal rewards)
- Gold: SGD $200/month ad credit
- Platinum: SGD $500/month ad credit

**Why this works:**
- ✅ They spend it on **advertising their own errands** (not team rewards)
- ✅ When they use ad credit, they still pay Stripe fees
- ✅ Ad campaigns stay on platform (not external redemptions)
- ✅ Creates engagement (they log in to use credits)
- ✅ Drives more errand visibility (helps platform)

---

## ✅ SOLUTION 3: Make EP Part of Premium (Tier-Locked)

Change positioning:
- **Free users:** Can earn EP from activity (50-500/month)
- **Silver:** Can also **receive** EP gifts from their company (opt-in, not automatic)
- **Gold:** Unlock advanced gifting + bulk rewards
- **Platinum:** Unlimited gifting + rewards automation

**This way:**
- EP isn't a subscription cost
- Companies can choose to gift or not
- You're not on the hook for "monthly allowance"

---

## ✅ SOLUTION 4: Raise Prices Significantly

If you want to keep EP allowances:

| Tier | Current | Suggested | EP/Month | Economics |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Silver** | $24 | **$49** | 300 EP | Break-even |
| **Gold** | $69 | **$149** | 1,000 EP | Profitable |
| **Platinum** | $129 | **$299** | 2,500 EP | Highly profitable |

**Problem:** Prices get expensive, might be hard to sell

---

## 📊 MY RECOMMENDATION

**Hybrid Approach:**

| Tier | Price | Commission | What They Get |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Silver** | $29/month | 18% | Small team tools + $30/month EP gifting *option* |
| **Gold** | $79/month | 17% | Growth tools + $100/month EP gifting *option* |
| **Platinum** | $149/month | 15% | Enterprise tools + $300/month EP gifting *option* |

**Key change: "Option" not "Automatic"**

This means:
- ✅ Subscription is profitable on its own
- ✅ Companies can gift EP if they want (you still break even)
- ✅ You're not forced to give away SGD $6K/year per Platinum customer
- ✅ Prices are fair for the massive features you maintain

---

## 🎯 What's Really Happening

Your subscriptions aren't just about **commission savings** (that's what YOU give them).

**They're about:**
1. **Team coordination** (worth SGD $100+/month)
2. **Financial reports** (worth SGD $200+/month)
3. **Payroll automation** (worth SGD $500+/month)
4. **Less manual work** (priceless)

**The EP is just a bonus on top**, not the main reason they subscribe.
